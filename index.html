Pokemon Pokedex



The Pokemon Poke-Dex sports 150 different pokemon. A user can select a pokemon and obtain information about each pokemon and add new Pokemon to their Poke-Dex The functionality of this app is simple learning of manipulating data without a true database. There are features replicating the CRUD methodology.

These instructions will help you see the app, should you not wish to download a copy of the project.You can access the respository here: SSJassal WDIR Projects Repository
Should you want to see the code and modify, these instructions will get you a copy of the project up and running on your local machine for development and testing purposes. Please clone my repository for Pokedex App from there you will find need to run npm install to install all necessary modules. You’ll want to ensure the server.js file holds the port you wish to run the app. You will then need to start the nodemon server. You can then run the app from http://localhost:port-given.

Soniya Jassal Bio

Soniya has 13 years of experience working in the Federal Market with backgrounds ranging in Web Development, full range Software Development Life Cycle, and Enterprise Architecture.  Soniya has a focus in Enterprise Architecture and currently works to support the Navy N1, HHS Grants.gov, Internal Revenue Service, Solution Architecture Development.  In the past, she has support the Department of Veteran Affairs (VA) Architecture, Strategy & Design Team implementing an integrated Enterprise Architecture with Segment federation, along with the support of an integrated tool suite to support the strategy, mission and goals of VA.  Soniya has also worked in support of Military Heath Systems(MHS) Office of the Chief Information Officer Enterprise Architecture Division (OCIO EAD) where she authored the Integration-Federation Strategy to capture the holistic view of the MHS Enterprise Architecture.  She has lead compliance and milestone reviews in support of the Investment Review Committee (IRC) and provided Subject Matter Expert (SME) reviews.  Soniya has also support the Enterprise Architecture Committee (EAC) Board for validation of MHS Enterprise Architecture artifacts.  Soniya has completed a Bachelor's in Information Networking Degree at the University of Minnesota and is currently completing her Master's Degree in Information Systems Engineering with emphasis in Cyber Security at Johns Hopkins Whiting Schools of Engineering.  In her personal time Soniya is active with her Fraternity Alumnae Club of Pi Beta Phi raising money and books through fundraising for literacy, investing time with the Girl Scouts of America, traveling, attending music concerts and spending time with her family.
